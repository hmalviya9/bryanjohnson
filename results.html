08b · Sunday batch-prep

90 minutes on Sunday = 7 weekday wins.

The single highest-leverage habit on this page. Protein and fibre are the two things Indians underhit — both are fixed by prep, not willpower.

BATCH 01

Moong sprouts

~10 min Sun + 24h passive
  1. Rinse 150g moong dal, soak 8h overnight.
  2. Drain, tie loosely in wet muslin.
  3. Hang in colander 18–24h at room temp.
  4. Refrigerate Mon morning. Eats 4 breakfasts.
BATCH 02

Overnight-oats jars

~15 min Sun
  1. 5 glass jars × (40g oats + 150ml milk/curd + 1 tbsp chia + chopped amla/jamun + dates for sweet).
  2. Lid, refrigerate. Lasts 5 days.
  3. Grab one per weekday morning. Zero cook-time.
BATCH 03

Home ghee from butter

~20 min Sun
  1. 500g Amul salted butter in heavy-bottom pan, medium-low flame.
  2. Foam → clear golden in 15 min. Milk solids turn brown (not black).
  3. Cool 5 min. Strain through muslin into jar.
  4. Yields ~380g. ₹250 vs ₹550 retail A2.
BATCH 04

Freeze jamun + amla (in season)

~20 min Sun
  1. Wash 500g, pat fully dry.
  2. Spread single-layer on tray, freeze 2h.
  3. Transfer to airtight bag — lasts 6 months.
  4. Jamun Jun–Aug; amla Oct–Feb. Stock when ₹40/kg, not ₹300.
BATCH 05

Home curd (dahi)

~10 min + 8h passive
  1. Boil 500ml full-fat milk, cool to wrist-warm (~43°C).
  2. Whisk in 1 tsp existing curd as starter.
  3. Cover, leave 8h in warm corner (oven with light on works).
  4. Refrigerate. Daily starter for next batch.
BATCH 06

Salad base (chop-once)

~15 min Sun
  1. Chop 1 cucumber, 1 onion, 2 tomato, handful coriander into airtight container.
  2. Add 1 tbsp lemon juice (preserves + adds vit C).
  3. Refrigerate. 3-day shelf life.
  4. Mon–Wed lunches: scoop 1 cup, add protein-of-day.
09 · The Free-5 plate rule

If you remember one thing from this page.

Works for every meal, every diet, every budget. No app, no tracking, no measuring. Build every plate in these proportions and you'll hit ~80% of what the supplement stack above is trying to fix.

½ plate
vegetables
+ greens
¼ protein
dal / eggs / fish / paneer
¼ grain
ragi / brown rice / roti
  • ½ plate vegetables & greens. Any colour, any cook method. Methi, palak, lauki, bhindi, tomato, gajar — all count.
  • ¼ plate protein. Target 1g/kg/day. Indians under-hit by ~30g average. Dal alone won't do it past age 35.
  • ¼ plate whole grain. Ragi > brown rice > atta > white rice. Skip this quarter on keto days.
  • 1 thumb of fat. Ghee, cold-pressed mustard, EVOO, or 10g nuts. Not refined sunflower/palm blend.
  • 1 fist of fermented. Dahi, buttermilk, dhokla, dosa batter, kimchi if you have it. Daily, non-negotiable.

Top 7 Indian longevity foods

  1. AmlaHighest natural vit C by weight. ₹40/kg in season.
  2. JamunAnthocyanins + glycemic blunting. Freeze in Jul.
  3. Moong sproutsCheap protein + lysine. Raw or tadka.
  4. Curd / dahiDaily fermented — single best gut habit.
  5. Cold-pressed mustard oilMUFA + ALA. FSSAI 2022 caps erucic ≤5%.
  6. Turmeric + pepperCurcumin bioavailability × 20 with piperine.
  7. RagiCalcium density rivals dairy. Roti / porridge / dosa.
