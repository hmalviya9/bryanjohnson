Section S2

How to read the badges

read me first
Verified

Tier-1 source cited (peer-reviewed RCT, meta-analysis, or major cohort). The claim and the number both match the source. Acts on the claim at your discretion.

Flagged

Directionally true but with material caveats — dose, subgroup, cohort, bioavailability, seasonality, or Indian-cohort-specific genetic context. Read the caveat before acting.

Unverified

Mentioned in traditional texts or popular literature; we have not yet sourced a tier-1 citation. Do not act on unverified items without your physician.

Scope honesty · S3 This is a floor, not a ceiling. It is not medical advice. It cannot substitute for a physician, a therapist, or prescribed medication. Pillar 1 · Section S4

Food — ancestral plates

p1 · food

Every indigenous culture that outlived a famine did it on the same short list: whole grains, legumes, leafy greens, a fermented ferment, a seasonal fruit, a nut or seed, a spice, and an oil that didn't come from a factory. The Blue Zones, the Hunza valley, the Okinawan Ryukyu kitchen, the Keralan sadya, the Sardinian shepherd's table, the Andean campesino — different continents, same grammar. The cards below are pulled from that grammar.

India · S. Asia Verified
Amla
Phyllanthus emblica · Indian gooseberry
Vit C · Chromium · Polyphenols
Highest vitamin-C density of any common Indian fruit (~600 mg / 100 g, heat-stable). Anchor fruit of Charaka's Rasayana category. Lowers fasting glucose in small RCTs; chromium content supports insulin sensitivity.
Contraindications
Anticoagulant therapy (warfarin) — amla potentiates effect. Severe hyperacidity / active ulcer. Pre-surgery: stop 14 days prior.
Sources
Akhtar MS 2011, Int J Food Sci Nutr · Usharani P 2013, Diabetes Metab Syndr Obes · Charaka Samhita, Sūtrasthāna Ch. 27.
Last verified 2026-04-22 · claim food_amla
India · W. Africa Flagged
Moringa
Moringa oleifera · drumstick / sahjan
Iron · Magnesium · Quercetin
Leaves carry an unusual iron-to-plant-matter ratio for a green; magnesium content supports neurotransmitter synthesis (GABA, serotonin). Used in South-Indian sambar and in West-African sauces for centuries.
Contraindications
Pregnancy (root/bark especially — abortifacient traditionally). Powdered supplements have been flagged for heavy-metal load in some FSSAI audits — prefer fresh leaves or tested brands.
Sources
Gopalakrishnan L 2016, Food Science & Human Wellness · Stohs SJ 2015, Phytother Res. FLAGGED because mental-health endpoint data is thin; nutrient data is solid.
Last verified 2026-04-22 · claim food_moringa
Japan Verified
Nattō
Fermented soybean · Bacillus subtilis natto
Vit K2 (MK-7) · Probiotics · Nattokinase
Single richest dietary source of Vitamin K2 MK-7 — the form that actually reaches arteries and bone. Associated in Japanese cohorts with lower all-cause mortality (Katanoda 2017). Fermented → gut-brain axis hit.
Contraindications
Warfarin therapy (K2 interaction). Severe soy allergy. Acquired taste — start small.
Sources
Katanoda K 2017, Eur J Epidemiol (Takayama cohort) · Nakamura Y 2020, Heart.

India (staple) · Ethiopia (origin) Verified
Ragi
Eleusine coracana · finger millet
Calcium · Tryptophan · Fiber
Highest calcium content of any common grain (~344 mg / 100 g). Tryptophan → serotonin precursor; low-GI slow carb. South Karnataka and Tamil Nadu weaning food; Ethiopian injera origin region.
Contraindications
Kidney stone history (oxalate) — pair with calcium-rich curd. Hypothyroidism — limit raw, prefer fermented/sprouted.
Sources
Chandra D 2016, J Food Sci Technol · ICRISAT millet dossier 2023.

W. Africa Verified
Fonio
Digitaria exilis · the "hungry rice"
Methionine · Cystine · Slow carb
Grows in 6–8 weeks on poor soil — famine staple of the Sahel for 5,000 years. One of the few plant grains with strong methionine + cystine. Low GI, gluten-free. Drought-resilient; climate-hedge food.
Contraindications
None material. Rinse thoroughly — traditional processing removes chaff debris.
Sources
Cruz JF 2016, CIRAD monograph · Jideani AIO 2011, Comp Rev Food Sci.

Chenopodium quinoa · Inca staple
Complete protein · Lysine · Mg
One of very few plant foods carrying all nine essential amino acids at useful ratios. Cultivated by the Inca for ~5,000 years. Rotates with amaranth in Andean altiplano diets.

Saponin coating — rinse before cooking. Oxalate load — moderate intake if kidney-stone history.

Amaranthus cruentus · rajgira / chaulai
Lysine · Iron · Squalene
Rajgira ladoo is a fasting-food across India for a reason — it holds up nutritionally where grain-rice does not. Aztec sacred crop; squalene content unusual for a seed.

Andean Peru · Bolivia Flagged

Lepidium meyenii · adaptogen
Adaptogen · Cortisol balance
Root used for 2,000+ years at 4,000 m Andean altitude. Small trials show effects on energy, libido, mild mood; mechanism is multi-factorial, probably HPA-axis.

Seaweeds
Kombu · Wakame · Nori
Iodine · L-glutamate · Fucoidans
Dietary iodine sufficiency is the unglamorous reason the Japanese thyroid profile differs from the American one. Kombu in dashi, wakame in miso, nori in sushi — every meal touches the thyroid.

Fiber · Complete protein (pair) · Mg
Beans + corn eaten together form a complete protein — the Mesoamerican invariant for 5,000 years. Loma Linda Adventist cohort legume data: every 20 g/day of legumes → ~8% lower mortality.

India · S.E. Asia Verified
Turmeric
Curcuma longa · haldi
Curcumin · BDNF · Anti-inflammatory
Curcumin modulates BDNF and inflammatory cytokines; meta-analysis shows modest antidepressant effect at 500–1,000 mg curcuminoid / day (Ng 2017). Bioavailability jumps ~2,000% with piperine (black pepper).
Contraindications
Anticoagulant therapy. Gallstones / bile-duct obstruction. High-dose supplements, not dietary turmeric, carry hepatotoxicity reports — stick to food doses unless a physician says otherwise.

Adansonia digitata
Vit C · Prebiotic fiber · Calcium

Chia + Sacha Inchi
Salvia hispanica · Plukenetia volubilis

Pillar 2 · Section S5

Sleep — ragas & Ayurveda

p2 · sleep

Indian classical music encodes a 24-hour emotional clock — prahar — where specific ragas belong to specific hours. Evening and late-night ragas are mostly in the shanta (peace), karuna (compassion), and gambhir (majestic-calm) rasas. Used as a wind-down listening protocol — not as a cure for insomnia — they do what slow-tempo instrumental music does best: downshift the sympathetic nervous system. Below: twelve ragas from that tradition, and an Ayurvedic sleep kit that pairs with them.

S5c · Sleep hygiene — the non-negotiable five

01

Same time, every day.

Within ±30 min sleep and wake, seven days. Weekend drift is the single biggest circadian saboteur.

Walker 2017 · Wittmann 2006 (social jetlag)
02

Bedroom is 18–20°C.

Core body temperature drop is a necessary signal. Hot bedrooms = fragmented REM.

Okamoto-Mizuno 2012, J Physiol Anthropol
03

Blackout + silence, or pink noise.

Eye-mask + earplugs work as well as $2,000 blackout shades. Pink noise improves slow-wave in small studies.

Papalambros 2017, Front Hum Neurosci
04

No screens 90 min pre-bed.

Not mainly about blue light — mainly about content. A feed is a stimulant regardless of wavelength.

Hale 2015, Sleep Med Rev
05

Morning sun within 30 min of waking.

10 minutes outdoors is the strongest zeitgeber available. Fixes sleep timing without a pill.

Blume 2019, Somnologie

Pillar 3 · Section S6

Movement — ancient & modern

p3 · movement

The exercise literature converges on the same prescription every decade: move daily, lift something heavy twice a week, break a sweat, and get your heart rate up past conversation-pace for at least 75 minutes a week. Every modern branded protocol — HIIT, Tabata, Wim Hof, Animal Flow, Primal Movement, Box Breathing — is a repackaging of something older. The cards below show the originals and the packaging.

S6a · Eight asanas with mental-health evidence

Balasana

Child's pose · बालासन60–120 sec · passive

Forward fold on knees, forehead on floor. Parasympathetic reset; good between poses or at day's end.

Shavasana

Corpse pose · शवासन5–15 min · rest

The hardest pose, paradoxically. Full supine relaxation, breath observed not controlled. Foundation of yoga nidra.

Setu Bandhasana

Bridge pose · सेतु बंधासन30–60 sec × 3

Supine hip-lift. Opens chest, stretches psoas, associated with mild antidepressant effect in yoga-depression RCTs (Cramer 2013).

Viparita Karani

Legs-up-the-wall · विपरीत करणी5–10 min · passive inversion

Mild inversion, no skill required. Calms nervous system; good for pre-sleep paired with Yaman raga.

Adho Mukha Svanasana

Downward dog · अधोमुख श्वानासन30–60 sec × 3

Full-body lengthener. Shoulder, hamstring, calf in one shape. Anchor pose of most Vinyasa flows.

Uttanasana

Standing forward fold · उत्तानासन30–60 sec

Decompresses spine; cheap anxiety reset — head below heart for 30 sec drops sympathetic tone measurably.

Sukhasana + breath-watch

Easy sitting · सुखासन5–20 min

Cross-legged upright seat; simplest meditation posture. The pose is the point — sit, notice, do nothing.

Vrikshasana

Tree pose · वृक्षासन30–60 sec each side

Single-leg balance. Balance training is under-rated for neurological ageing; 30 seconds is harder than it looks past 50.

S6b · Six pranayama, with dose + hard contraindications

Nadi Shodhana

Alternate-nostril · नाड़ी शोधन 5–10 min · daily AM or PM

4 sec in (L), 4 sec hold, 4 sec out (R), switch. Well-tolerated; HRV and calm markers improve in small RCTs.

Contraindications
Acute sinusitis (discomfort). Safe for most populations. Stop if dizzy.

Bhramari

Bee-breath · भ्रामरी 5–10 rounds · 2 min

Humming exhale. Raises nitric-oxide output ~15×; vagal stimulation via laryngeal vibration. Cheap evening sympathetic-off switch.

Contraindications
Active ear infection. Severe tinnitus (may amplify perception of ringing).

Ujjayi

Ocean-breath · उज्जायी Continuous · during asana or standalone 5 min

Slight glottal constriction on in + out breath. Trains slow, resistance breathing. Used throughout Ashtanga practice.

Contraindications
Uncontrolled asthma. Sore throat. Back off if light-headed.

Kapalbhati

Skull-shining · कपालभाति 30–60 exhales × 3 rounds · AM only

Short sharp exhales, passive inhale. Energising; not a wind-down practice. Morning only.

Contraindications — hard stops
Hypertension · CAD · pregnancy · hernia · recent abdominal surgery · retinal conditions · vertigo. Do not attempt during menstruation.

Anulom-Vilom

Channel-cleansing · अनुलोम विलोम 5–10 min · daily

Simpler cousin of Nadi Shodhana, without retention. Good entry pranayama; safe for most including older adults.

Contraindications
None material at beginner dose. Stop if dizzy.

Sheetali

Cooling breath · शीतली 5–10 rounds · hot weather / evening

Inhale through rolled tongue, exhale through nose. Cools body and mind; traditional prescription for pitta / summer heat.

Contraindications
Low blood pressure. Asthma (cold-air sensitivity). Winter — skip or shorten.

Pillar 4 · Section S7

Attention — content & connection

p4 · attention

Nothing in the $2M protocol addresses the lever that is quietly doing the most damage: attention-economy optimisation + social isolation. Holt-Lunstad's 2015 meta-analysis puts loneliness mortality impact at roughly a 15-cigarette/day equivalent. Hunt 2018's RCT showed a 30-min/day social-media cap materially reduces depression and loneliness in three weeks. The protocol below is the cheapest pillar. It may also be the highest-leverage one.

S7a · The digital floor — non-negotiables

01

First 90 min · no phone.

No feed, no email, no news. The morning cortisol window belongs to sunlight, water, movement, and one human if possible.

Derks 2016, J Occup Health Psychol
02

Last 90 min · no screens.

Not the blue light — the content. A feed is a stimulant regardless of wavelength. Read a physical book.

Hale 2015, Sleep Med Rev
03

Phone out of the bedroom.

Charges in another room, full stop. Buy a $10 alarm clock. This single change is the biggest lift any of us will make.

Dunlop 2021, JMIR Form Res
04

Grayscale mode on.

iOS Accessibility → Display Filters → Color Filters → Grayscale. Kills ~40% of reward-loop pull in self-report.

Holte 2021, Mobile Media & Communication
05

Notifications: whitelist only.

Phone calls + messages from a named list. Everything else silenced. Review quarterly.

Stothart 2015, JEP: HPP
06

Sunday digital sabbath.

One day a week, phone in a drawer. Start with two hours if 24 is too much. Ratchet up.

Mirbabaie 2020, Electron Mark

S7b · The content diet

Read > watch.

Reading is active; watching is passive. Default to the book when both are options.

Long-form > short-form.

A 90-minute documentary outperforms 90 one-minute reels on every metric that matters — retention, mood, stress hormones.

No news before breakfast.

Nothing in the news cycle is more urgent than your blood sugar and your circadian rhythm at 7 a.m.

One book at a time.

Five books half-read = zero books read. Serial, not parallel.

Quarterly feed audit.

Once every three months: unfollow anyone whose content made you feel worse than it made you smarter.

Curate, don't consume.

Every feed is editable. Every app is deletable. Every notification is negotiable.

S7c · Body defaults — sun, earth, heat, cold

10 min morning sun

Within 30 min of waking · skin exposed

Strongest circadian anchor available. Vitamin D bonus. Works even through light cloud.

15–20 min barefoot outdoor walk

Grass · sand · clean soil · daily

"Earthing" physiological effect is FLAGGED (Chevalier 2012, small N); the walk itself is not. Do it for the walk; the grounding is an anecdotal bonus.

Sauna · 4–7×/week · 19 min avg

Traditional or IR

Finnish KIHD cohort: ~40% all-cause mortality drop, ~50% fatal CVD drop. Cohort-flagged (middle-aged Finnish men) — generalisability unproven but trend robust.

Cold exposure · 2–5 min · 2–4×/week

11–15°C plunge or cold shower

Mood lift, brown-fat activation. Hard stops: uncontrolled hypertension, arrhythmia, Raynaud's. Never alone in open water.

S7d · Connection defaults — the mortality equivalent of quitting smoking

One meal/day shared · phones off.

Phones off the table. One proper conversation beats ten distracted ones. Holt-Lunstad's mortality number sits here.

Holt-Lunstad 2015, Perspectives Psych Sci

Weekly call · elders.

Parents, grandparents, older relatives. 20 minutes. A small amount of weekly effort outperforms a large amount of occasional effort.

Carstensen 1999, American Psychologist

One "third place" · recurring.

Not work. Not home. A class, sport league, spiritual gathering, volunteer crew. Once a week minimum.

Oldenburg 1989, The Great Good Place

Long walk · old friend · monthly.

No agenda, no phone, no screen. 60–90 min. The single most under-rated mental-health intervention of the decade.

Clark 2018, J Epidemiol Community Health

Section S9 · Safety

Contraindications — read before you start.

do not skip

Populations that must clear items with a physician first

  • Pregnancy / nursing: Ashwagandha, Jatamansi, Shankhpushpi, Moringa root/bark, high-dose nutmeg, Kapalbhati, cold-plunge ≤ 10°C, maca, sauna > 15 min.
  • Anticoagulants (warfarin, DOACs): Amla, turmeric supplements, nattō (K2), ashwagandha, ginkgo-family interactions.
  • Thyroid conditions: Ashwagandha (caution with hyper- and Hashimoto), maca (goitrogen if raw), seaweed excess, brahmi (meds interaction).
  • Autoimmune (lupus, RA, MS): Ashwagandha, high-dose turmeric supplements, Wim Hof / Tummo (cold exposure can flare some conditions).
  • Hypertension (uncontrolled) / CAD / arrhythmia: Kapalbhati, inversions, cold plunge, sauna without clearance, high-dose caffeine.
  • South Asian lipid profile (elevated ApoB / Lp(a)): High A2 ghee intake, mustard-oil daily cooking at large volume — see dossier S9.
  • Eating-disorder history: No fasting protocols, no strict macro counting, no "perfect plate" framing. The pillar is food, not restriction.
  • Psychiatric medication (SSRIs, MAOIs, lithium): St John's Wort (not in this module, noted here), high-dose turmeric, ashwagandha — all need clearance.
  • Children < 12: No adaptogens, no supplements of any kind without pediatrician. Pillars 1 + 4 apply fully; Pillars 2 + 3 at child-appropriate dose.
