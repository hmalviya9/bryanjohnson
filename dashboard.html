Critical — mustard oil + ghee is not an EVOO substitute safety
A common suggestion is to swap EVOO for cold-press mustard oil + A2 ghee. This is chemically incoherent: ghee is ~62% saturated fat, mustard+ghee combined does not deliver the oleocanthal / oleuropein / hydroxytyrosol phenolics responsible for EVOO's anti-inflammatory effect in PREDIMED. Ghee has different benefits (butyrate), not EVOO-equivalent ones. If phenolic polyphenols are your target, use Indian EVOO.
Mustard oil — erucic acid warning critical
Traditional Indian kachi-ghani mustard oil contains 30–54% erucic acid. FSSAI permits up to 45% in India; EU limit is 2%; US/Canada ban high-erucic for edible use (regulated since 1956). EFSA sets tolerable daily intake at 7 mg/kg body weight. If using mustard oil, specify low-erucic cultivars (Pusa Double Zero, Pusa Mustard 30) or limit to 1 tsp/day cooking use. AITC "anti-inflammatory" claim is a preclinical / topical surrogate for inflammation, not a validated dietary anti-inflammatory.

Moringin ≠ sulforaphane biology
Moringa's moringin (from glucomoringin) is a different isothiocyanate than broccoli sprouts' sulforaphane (from glucoraphanin). Moringin is more stable and on a molar basis can be more potent for Nrf2 induction, but they bind different protein targets at the margins and clinical endpoints differ. If you specifically want sulforaphane dose, broccoli sprouts remain the gold standard. Moringa is a legitimate ITC source — just not the same molecule.
Contraindications — moringa safety
Moringa root and bark contain alkaloids with uterotonic activity — avoid in pregnancy. Leaves are considered safer but still cautious in 1st trimester. Powdered moringa from uncontrolled sources has shown heavy-metal load (Pb, Cd) in Indian market surveys — prefer FSSAI-licensed brands with batch testing.

Where the NAD+ actually rises biology
Aerobic + resistance training upregulates NAMPT (rate-limiting NAD+ salvage enzyme) and raises skeletal muscle NAD+ ~25–30% — this is the tissue that matters for longevity endpoints. FDA reversed its 2022 "not a dietary supplement" position in Sept 2025 (NDI status), which does not extend to FSSAI. "NMN is banned in India" — circulating in some commentary — is false; "NMN is approved in India" would also be misleading.

Juice is not "Western dilution" — the biology reference

08 · Indian genetic context

Your biology is not Finnish.

Four genetic variants differ meaningfully in Indian populations from the Western cohorts most longevity studies use. These shift what "optimal dose" means. Not destiny — but context.

FADS1 / FADS2

ALA → EPA conversion

Impacts the walnut swap
South Asians carry elevated frequency of the FADS1 rs174537 "derived" allele. Nominally this means higher desaturase activity and slightly better ALA→EPA conversion than Europeans. But absolute conversion remains low: 5–8% ALA→EPA, <0.5% ALA→DHA. Walnuts help; they do not substitute for direct EPA/DHA in high-CVD-risk individuals or pregnancy.
Indian allele frequency · elevated vs EUR · Kothapalli 2016 (PMID 26432412)
OCTN1 / SLC22A4

Ergothioneine retention

Impacts the mushroom swap
Ergothioneine is taken up into cells via OCTN1 transporter (SLC22A4 gene). Loss-of-function variants blunt tissue retention — mushrooms still raise plasma ergothioneine but cellular delivery varies. Indian-population allele frequencies for OCTN1 variants are not well characterised in the published literature — an open gap.
Status · allele frequency unpublished for Indian cohorts
APOE ε4

Saturated fat sensitivity

Impacts ghee / dairy / sauna
APOE ε4 carriers show elevated LDL-C response to dietary saturated fat and elevated Alzheimer's risk. Indian ε4 frequency ~8–15% (varies by region; lower than NW European). If unknown, cap A2 ghee at 1 tsp/day; if known ε4+, prefer Indian EVOO as primary fat. Testing in India (2026): 23andMe no longer ships to India; domestic options include MapMyGenome Hyderabad (~₹9,900), Positive Bioscience (~₹7,500–15,000), and Gene Support. Discuss testing implications (insurance, family disclosure) before ordering — ε4 result cannot be un-learned.
Indian ε4 frequency · 8–15% · PMC7734559 (2020)
LPA (Lp(a))

Genetic CVD risk, diet-refractory

Impacts labs panel priority
South Asians show elevated Lp(a) prevalence compared to most cohorts. Lp(a) is ~90% genetically determined at population level (individual exceptions in post-menopause and hormonal states) and does not respond to diet, exercise, statins, or most lifestyle levers. It is the strongest independent genetic CVD risk factor. Test once, then retest if: you start lipid-lowering therapy, you reach post-menopause, or a Phase 3 Lp(a)-targeting therapy (pelacarsen, olpasiran) becomes prescribable. Request results in nmol/L (molar units) for international comparability; Indian labs mix nmol/L and mg/dL. If elevated, shift to aggressive ApoB lowering + PCSK9 inhibitors — protocol changes, not food choices.
Indian elevated-Lp(a) prevalence · ~25–35% at >50 mg/dL cutoff · Enas 2019 (Indian Heart J)
09 · Contraindications

Who should not do these.

Six populations for whom one or more swaps here are materially risky. Read this before you act on anything above.

Pregnancy

Avoid or consult physician

  • Ashwagandha — traditional abortifacient; avoid entirely
  • Moringa root / bark — uterotonic; leaves caution in T1
  • Sauna / IR blanket — hyperthermia >39°C linked to neural tube defects
  • NMN / resveratrol — no pregnancy safety data
  • CGM — safe, but glucose targets differ in gestational context
Uncontrolled hypertension · recent MI

Skip the sauna

  • Sauna — SBP >180 / DBP >110 causes pre-sauna surges; recent MI (<6 weeks), unstable angina, severe aortic stenosis — avoid
  • Alcohol + sauna — doubles sudden cardiac death risk (Kenttämies 2008)
  • HBOT — uncontrolled HTN adds barotrauma risk
Hyperthyroidism / autoimmune

Ashwagandha caution

  • Ashwagandha — can raise T3/T4; caution in hyperthyroidism
  • Autoimmune (Hashimoto's, RA, lupus) — theoretical immunomodulation; discuss with physician
  • Moringa — mild immunomodulation in preclinical models
Elevated LDL / ApoB / CAD

Saturated fat discipline

  • Traditional mustard oil — 30–54% erucic acid; banned for edible use in EU/US. Use low-erucic cultivars only
  • A2 ghee — cap 1 tsp/day; ghee >62% saturated fat
  • Coconut oil — similar caution; not a substitute for EVOO phenolics
Non-diabetic CGM wearers

Over-interpretation risk

  • CGM in non-diabetics — normal postprandial spikes (to 140 mg/dL) are not pathological
  • Anxiety-driven diet restriction is a documented adverse effect
  • Use as trend tool for protocol experiments, not a real-time discipline device
Children · elderly · heat-acclimated summer users

Thermoregulation load

  • Sauna / IR — children <12 (immature thermoregulation); reduce frequency at Indian summer WBGT >28°C
  • Severe dehydration, diuretic therapy — pre-hydrate or skip
  • Dose sauna sessions to Finnish cohort = 80–100°C steam > IR blanket ≠ direct effect transfer

10b · Buying safely in India

What the label doesn't tell you.

Indian retail carries documented adulteration and fraud across most of the ingredients recommended on this page. Swapping to "the cheapest brand you can find" is a false economy. Everything below is FSSAI / CSE / published-survey territory — not conspiracy.

Turmeric · lead chromate
Stanford 2021 · FSSAI 2023
documented Pb contamination in multiple states
Lead chromate is added to brighten colour. Prefer AGMARK-graded turmeric, single-origin (Erode/Sangli), or brands publishing heavy-metal COA. Whole rhizome grated at home is safer than powder. If uncertain, send a sample to an FSSAI-empanelled lab.
Olive oil · refined-as-EVOO fraud
Routine globally
including Indian retail
Verify brand on the International Olive Council approved list, check acidity <0.8% on the label, and prefer dark-glass bottles with a harvest date (not just "best before"). "Pure olive oil" and "olive pomace oil" are refined grades — not EVOO. Named brands anywhere on this page are illustrative of retail availability, not endorsements.
Honey · sugar-syrup adulteration
CSE 2020 · 10 of 13 brands
tested failed NMR fingerprint
Centre for Science and Environment 2020 report flagged sugar-syrup adulteration in 10 of 13 tested Indian honey brands. Prefer raw local honey with NMR or C4-sugar COA. Note: this dossier doesn't include honey in the daily receipt — mentioned here so readers don't back-fill with a compromised product.
Ashwagandha + moringa powder · heavy metals
ASU survey data
Pb/Cd in uncontrolled herbal powders
Unbranded bulk herbal powders have shown Pb, Cd, and microbial load in Indian surveys. Prefer FSSAI-licensed brands with batch-level heavy-metal and microbiological COA (Ixoreal/KSM-66, branded moringa e.g. Kapiva, Organic India) over loose market powder.
Milk / ghee · adulterants + A2 claims
FSSAI surveys
detergent, urea, vegetable fat
Historical FSSAI surveys have flagged adulterants in loose dairy supply. Branded A2 (e.g. Akshayakalpa) or pasteurised supply chains reduce risk. A2 genotype claims are brand-asserted — most Indian native breeds are A2-dominant but not 100%; the health differential between A1 and A2 milk remains contested in the literature.
Supplements · FSSAI licence + third-party COA
Rule of thumb
FSSAI licence no. · batch COA · lab name
Any supplement on this page (NMN, omega-3, creatine, vitamin D3+K2, magnesium glycinate) — check the FSSAI licence number on pack and ask for a third-party batch COA if the brand website doesn't publish one. Indian nutraceutical manufacturing is governed by FSSAI Nutraceutical Regulations 2022; compliance is self-attested and batch-variability is the real-world risk.

12 · The Free 5

The five that actually move mortality.

These five interventions dominate preventable mortality in every large cohort study. None requires a Blueprint SKU. Hazard ratios come from different cohorts measuring different comparators; they cannot be summed. The single best composite anchor is EPIC-Norfolk (Khaw et al. 2008, PMID 18184055): adults practising all four behaviours (non-smoker, physically active, moderate alcohol, ≥5 servings fruit/veg) showed ~4× lower all-cause mortality over 11 years versus adults practising zero. That factor is the honest "why bother with the Free 5" number.

~50%
Don't use tobacco
Mid-life cessation halves continued-smoking hazard (Doll & Peto 2004, cessation-vs-continuing — not vs never). Scope for India: includes cigarettes, bidi, gutka, khaini and other smokeless forms — any-tobacco prevalence is ~28% per GATS-2 (MoHFW 2017), and bidi/smokeless carry comparable or higher mortality than cigarettes.
DOLL & PETO · BMJ 2004 · GATS-2 India 2017
~15–20%
Control weight
RCT meta-analysis: RR 0.85 from intentional weight loss in obese adults. The larger headline numbers you see elsewhere usually conflate BMI-risk with weight-loss-benefit — distinct claims.
PMC4368053 · 2015 META
~35%
Move daily
PURE study (130,000 people, 17 countries): high PA vs low PA HR 0.65. Dose-response robust across cohorts.
LANCET · PURE 2017
~25–30%
Control BP
SPRINT: ~27% ACM reduction intensive <120 vs standard <140 in high-CV-risk adults. No benefit in frail >75. Context-dependent.
SPRINT · NEJM 2015
~21%
Eat plant-forward
MedDiet highest vs lowest adherence: RR 0.79 = 21% reduction in all-cause mortality.
ScienceDirect · 2022 META
The Load-Bearing Claim

Four healthy behaviours together associate with ~4× lower all-cause mortality (EPIC-Norfolk, 11-yr follow-up). Bryan Johnson's $2M buys margin, not foundation.

12b · What this dossier does not cover

The honest scope holes.

A food + supplement dossier cannot be a full longevity playbook. The interventions below are higher-leverage than most of the supplements we priced, and they are not covered on this page. If "~4× lower all-cause mortality from four healthy behaviours" is the anchor, these are the behaviours you still have to do after you finish reading.

Sleep — 7h consistent
HR ~0.88
Cappuccio 2010 meta-analysis
Sleep duration <6h or >9h both associate with higher mortality. Consistency of sleep/wake timing independently predicts outcomes. Mentioned on this page only as a Ring metric — it deserves its own intervention card.
Resistance training
HR ~0.85
Momma 2022, Br J Sports Med (PMID 35228201)
Muscle mass is the dominant predictor of functional independence past 65. DEXA measures it on this page; the intervention that changes it is not discussed. 2–3 sessions/week, progressive overload, protein ≥1.2 g/kg.
Vaccination (adult)
HR ~0.66
Nichol 2007 (flu, older adults)
Seasonal flu, COVID-19 boosters per current guidance, Tdap every 10 yr, shingles (≥50), pneumococcal (≥65 or comorbidity). Often zero cost after insurance; higher mortality impact than most supplements on this page.
Alcohol — the blind spot
"no safe level"
WHO 2023; Lancet 2022
Prior "J-curve" likely confounded by sick-quitter bias and genetic selection. Current WHO/ICMR guidance: no level of alcohol is safe for cancer risk. This page doesn't cost your drinks into the ₹96K — by far the biggest omission-of-scope.
Oral + periodontal health
HR ~1.25
periodontitis → CVD · Sanz 2020
Periodontal disease is an independent CVD risk factor. Indian adult dental-visit rate is <10%. A dental clean every 6 months out-performs many of the nutraceuticals priced here.
Social + mental health
HR ~0.50
Holt-Lunstad 2010 · isolation vs connected
Social isolation carries mortality risk comparable to smoking ~15 cigarettes/day. Depression, untreated anxiety, and sleep-disrupting mental-health conditions are higher-leverage than any supplement on this page.
Counter-evidence · dissenting voices

The strongest arguments against this page.

  • Peter Attia (The Drive, various episodes) — past the Free 5 and lab-guided basics (ApoB, Lp(a), HbA1c, VO₂ max, muscle mass), returns on additional supplementation are small. Most of Blueprint's stack is optimising beyond where returns are well-characterised.
  • NMN outcome RCTs are mixed (Yi et al. 2023; Irie et al. 2020; related systematic reviews) — NAD+ rises in blood but clinical endpoints (strength, gait speed, chair stand) show minimal or null benefit. "Raises a biomarker" ≠ "extends healthspan."
  • Compression of morbidity vs extension of lifespan (Fries 1980; Crimmins & Beltrán-Sánchez 2011) — population-level lifespan increases have not been matched by equivalent healthspan increases. Individual biomarker optimisation in healthy adults has not been shown to translate into longer or better years, only to higher likelihood of intermediate-surrogate improvements.
  • Comparator problem — none of the swaps on this page have been tested head-to-head against the Blueprint version in a randomised trial on a hard endpoint. Biology-target equivalence is plausible, not proven.
  • Regression to the mean + healthy-user bias — people who voluntarily follow a 21-item supplement stack differ on ~everything else (income, exercise, sleep, primary care access) from people who don't. Supplement-cohort observational studies systematically overstate effect size.

13 · Legal + regulatory

Disclaimers, by regulatory regime.

This page is educational. It is not medical advice. Every swap that touches an ingestible, a device, or a measurement falls under one or more Indian regulatory regimes. If you have questions about a specific recommendation, ask a physician licensed in your jurisdiction before acting.

Seven regimes that apply here

FSSAI Nutraceutical Regulations 2022
Dietary supplements sold in India are regulated under the Food Safety and Standards (Health Supplements, Nutraceuticals & Food for Special Dietary Use) Regulations 2022. NMN, ashwagandha extracts, omega-3s, and multivitamins are sold under this regime with self-attested compositional compliance. Therapeutic claims are restricted.
CDSCO / Medical Devices Rules 2017
CGMs, BP monitors, red-light therapy devices, and HBOT chambers sold for therapeutic use require CDSCO Class A/B/C/D registration depending on risk. Ring / wearable health trackers that do not claim medical diagnosis fall outside this regime. Verify registration before purchase.
ICMR-DBT National Guidelines for Stem Cell Research 2017
Stem-cell therapies, follistatin gene therapy, and most experimental interventions on Bryan Johnson's protocol are regulated under ICMR-DBT 2017 + CDSCO. Outside approved clinical trials, they are not accessible at retail in India.
AYUSH + Drugs and Magic Remedies Act 1954
Ashwagandha, moringa, amla, and other AYUSH-category ingredients are regulated by the Ministry of AYUSH. DMRA 1954 restricts claims of cure for listed conditions (diabetes, cancer, heart disease, obesity, etc.) in advertising.
FSSAI Health Claims Regulation 2022
Nutrient-function and disease-reduction claims on food packaging are restricted to FSSAI's approved list. Blog/website claims (like this page) are less restricted but must not "tend to deceive" per Consumer Protection Act 2019.
Consumer Protection Act 2019 + ASCI 2023 rules
Misleading advertisements are actionable. ASCI 2023 influencer guidelines require disclosure of material connection (affiliate links, gifted products). Named brands on this page are examples of Indian retail availability, not endorsements; the publisher carries no commercial relationship with any named entity as of April 2026. This posture is recertified annually. If a commercial relationship is later formed, ASCI-compliant inline disclosure will appear on the affected item.
DunedinPACE / epigenetic clock shipping legality
Sample shipping to TruDiagnostic (USA) is in a grey zone under Indian export of human biological samples rules. AIWO Health offers an in-India DunedinPACE equivalent via partner clinics.
