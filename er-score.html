§01 · What ER Measures

Three things at cellular level.

Your mitochondria don't just "make energy" — they signal stress, regulate hormones, and stamp themselves onto your skin, your mood, and the way you recover. ER is a proxy for how well that system is running today.

I.

Energy output

How quickly you recover from exertion. How much work you can stack before the quality of your focus drops. This is the visible side of ER — what Picard's lab measures as membrane potential and respiration rate.

II.

Stress buffer

Mitochondrial resilience to chronic cortisol, inflammation, and poor sleep. People with high ER shake off bad days. People with low ER compound them — premature greying, brain fog, hormonal drift.

III.

Signal clarity

Mitochondria are also communication organs — they release signals that tune immunity, mood, and circadian rhythm. Low ER scrambles the signal. High ER keeps the body legible to itself.

§03 · The Method

Where the numbers come from.

Two traditions informed this test. One is Martin Picard's mitochondrial psychobiology lab at Columbia, where researchers measure how stress, sleep, and social connection leave a chemical imprint on mitochondria. The other is Ayurvedic Dinacharya — a 3,000-year-old body of daily-rhythm practice that anticipated circadian biology by several millennia.

The inputs

Seven levers, not seventy.

Sleep, stress, exercise, social connection, diet quality, premature greying, cognitive clarity. Each is on a 5-point scale. The shortness is the point — more inputs would flatter the number without improving its signal.

The zones

Four domains, weighted.

Brain (cognition + stress + sleep). Metabolism (diet + sleep + exercise). Muscle (exercise + diet + sleep). Hormonal (social + stress + greying). Aggregate scores hide where the body is actually struggling — the zones surface it.

The archetypes

Five mito types.

Electron Surfer (90+), Steady Current (75–89), Cortex Burner (60–74), Ghost Reactor (40–59), Overclocker (<40). Not diagnoses — pattern-recognisers. The label tells you which kind of debt your mitochondria are carrying.

The caveats

What this is not.

ER is not a clinical test. It is a self-report inference, calibrated against public habits of fifty highly-documented people. Treat it as a mirror, not a diagnosis. For the real thing: DEXA, fasting insulin, VO2 max, HRV, and a doctor who reads them.
